Here's how "Campaign" works in SparqForge:

In simple terms, a Campaign is basically a single content creation project. Think of it like a "session" where you bring together your brand, a design template, and some assets, then let the AI generate social media content for you.

Here's the flow:

You set it up — Pick a brand, choose a template, optionally add reference images or a URL for design inspiration, and write a brief describing what you want.

AI generates content — The system creates platform-specific variants (Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter, TikTok) with both images and captions tailored to each platform.

You review and schedule — Look at the generated variants, tweak captions if needed, and schedule them to your social calendar.

A Campaign goes through these stages: Draft → Generating → Scheduled → Published.

So it's not a "campaign" in the traditional marketing sense (like a multi-week advertising push). It's more like a single batch of content generation — one creative run that produces a set of platform-specific posts from your inputs.

Great — now I have a full picture. Here are my thoughts:

Renaming "Campaign"
Since it's really a single content generation run, here are some alternatives that better describe what it actually is:

"Creation" — Simple, action-oriented. "Start a new Creation." The studio becomes "Creation Studio."
"Project" — Familiar and neutral. "Generate Project." Though it might feel too generic.
"Post Set" or "Post Run" — Very literal about the output. Could feel too informal.
"Generation" — Directly describes what's happening. "New Generation," "Generation Studio." Though it leans technical.
"Creative" — Common in agency/marketing speak for a single piece of work. "New Creative," "Creative Studio."
My recommendation would be "Creative" or "Creation" — both are intuitive for marketing teams and accurately describe the output without implying a multi-week advertising strategy.

Other Terminology Worth Reviewing
Looking at the rest of the app, most terms are actually solid, but a few might trip people up:

"Generation Packet" — Very technical. Something like "Preview" or "Summary" would be friendlier for non-technical team members.

"Compositing" — This is a video/design production term. Your team might understand "Brand Overlay" or "Finishing" more intuitively.

"Subject Reference" / "Style Reference" — These are clear to designers but might confuse others. Something like "Product Photo" and "Style Inspiration" could be more approachable.

"Brand DNA" — This one's actually good. It's evocative and most marketing folks will get it.

"Refine" — Clear and works well as-is.
